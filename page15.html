An Exploration to Impact Decision Making Abilities in Youth Through Sport

A capstone project by Jonathan Chimino

Abstract

This project aims to explore youth's development of decision making abilities, and how participation in sport may impact that process. The context is an after school sports program with 64 youth participants aged 7-11.


Pertinent information that was utilized to understand the complexities of impacting decision-making abilities through sport covered Ned Hermann’s Whole Brain Model, Sheena Iyengar’s Power of Choice, and David Kahneman’s Automaticity Theory. These frameworks provide insight to decision making processes and motivations that were applied to participants. As well, conditions and frameworks for positive youth development were provided by Nicholas Holt, Nancy Guerra, Ryan Hedstrom, Richard Catalano, and others. These various authors contributed frameworks that were created to modify or impact the development and functions of cognitive, moral, behavioral, and social life skills, but our study isolated decision making ability as a cognitive life skill. Although applied towards functions other than decision making ability, a common variable throughout all researched frameworks was the Teaching Personal and Social Responsibility (TPSR) model. Also common in the literature was factors that have impacted the development of responsibility, resilience, empathy, prosocial behaviors, and other developmental assets, or competencies.


Distinct variables for intervention methods had been identified through analysis of literature reviews. These distinct factors and strategies were extracted from relevant material and applied to a youth recreation program of individuals from 7-11 years old. Intervention methods included gamification techniques, providing developmental, external and internal, assets, challenges, motivation techniques and elements of positive youth development. Students were also profiled accordingly to the Learning Styles Inventory, and questioned to reveal indications of bias. Results were inconclusive but provided qualitative data to consider when implementing a more enhanced and capable and longitudinal strategy.
